<h2>The discovery framework</h2>
<p>Discovery has two goals: qualify the lead against our minimums, and understand the "why" behind their funding need. The "why" shapes your pitch and helps you connect capital to a real business outcome.</p>

<h2>Core qualifying questions</h2>
<ul>
  <li>"What type of business do you have, and how long have you been operating?"</li>
  <li>"What's your average monthly revenue looking like?"</li>
  <li>"Are you the owner or co-owner of the business?"</li>
  <li>"Do you have a business checking account in the company name?"</li>
</ul>

<h2>Need-uncovering questions</h2>
<ul>
  <li>"What are you looking to use the funding for?"</li>
  <li>"Is there a specific opportunity or project driving the timing?"</li>
  <li>"Have you explored other financing options? What happened?"</li>
  <li>"If you had the capital available tomorrow, what would you do first?"</li>
</ul>

<div class="tip-box">
  <p class="tip-label">Listen for the urgency</p>
  <p>The best leads have a specific, time-sensitive use case — a new contract they need to staff up for, an equipment purchase before busy season, inventory for a big order. Urgency accelerates decisions. If you find it, reflect it back to them throughout the call.</p>
</div>
