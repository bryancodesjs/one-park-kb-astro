<h2>Before you present</h2>
<p>Make sure you understand the offer details yourself before walking the client through it. Know the funded amount, factor rate, total payback, and estimated daily/weekly remittance.</p>

<h2>Structure for presenting</h2>
<ol>
  <li><strong>Anchor with the funded amount</strong> — lead with what they get</li>
  <li><strong>Explain the total payback</strong> — what they owe overall</li>
  <li><strong>Describe the remittance</strong> — how it gets paid back (daily/weekly % of revenue)</li>
  <li><strong>Tie it to their goal</strong> — connect the capital to what they told you they need it for</li>
  <li><strong>Ask for the next step</strong> — don't wait for them to drive</li>
</ol>

<div class="script-box">
  <p class="script-label">Full offer presentation template</p>
  <p>"So based on what we looked at, here's what we can offer you: <strong>[Amount]</strong> deposited directly into your business account. The total payback on that would be <strong>[Total Payback]</strong>, and that gets collected through a small daily percentage of your revenue — so you're not locked into a fixed payment that ignores how business is going. Given what you told me about [their specific goal], this should give you everything you need to [tie to their goal]. What questions do you have about the offer?"</p>
</div>

<div class="tip-box">
  <p class="tip-label">Don't apologize for the terms</p>
  <p>Present the offer with confidence. If you say "The rates are a little high but..." you've already planted doubt. Present the numbers factually, then pause and let the client respond. Confidence is contagious.</p>
</div>
