<h2>Purpose</h2>
<p>The first 30 seconds set the tone. Your goal is to be warm, professional, and immediately signal that this call is worth the client's time.</p>

<div class="script-box">
  <p class="script-label">Standard opening</p>
  <p>"Hi, this is [Your Name] calling from One Park Financial. I'm reaching out because you recently expressed interest in business funding — is now a good time to talk for a few minutes?"</p>
</div>

<div class="script-box">
  <p class="script-label">If they say yes — confirm and set the agenda</p>
  <p>"Perfect. I just want to ask you a few quick questions to see what options might be available for your business. It'll only take about 5 minutes, and I'll give you a straight answer on what we can do. Sound good?"</p>
</div>

<h2>If they say it's a bad time</h2>
<div class="script-box">
  <p class="script-label">Say this</p>
  <p>"No problem at all — I'll be quick. When is a good time to reach you? I want to make sure I have time set aside specifically for you."</p>
</div>

<div class="tip-box">
  <p class="tip-label">Pro tip</p>
  <p>Always ask "Is now a good time?" before launching into your pitch. It respects the client's time, reduces resistance, and if they say yes — they've subconsciously committed to the conversation.</p>
</div>

<h2>Call recording disclosure</h2>
<p>After the client confirms they can talk, include the required disclosure:</p>
<div class="script-box">
  <p class="script-label">Required</p>
  <p>"I also want to let you know that this call may be recorded for quality and training purposes — is that okay with you?"</p>
</div>
