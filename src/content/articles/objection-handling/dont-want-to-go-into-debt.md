<h2>What they're really saying</h2>
<p>The word "debt" carries emotional weight — especially for business owners who've seen others struggle. They're not objecting to capital; they're objecting to the risk they associate with fixed obligations. Revenue-based financing directly addresses that fear.</p>

<div class="script-box">
  <p class="script-label">Say this</p>
  <p>"I hear you — and that's exactly why revenue-based financing was built differently. You're not taking on a fixed loan payment that you owe regardless of how business goes. Your remittance adjusts with your revenue. Slower week? Smaller payment. Strong week? It pays down faster. You're always in control."</p>
</div>

<h2>Key differentiators to mention</h2>
<ul>
  <li>No fixed monthly payment that ignores your cash flow</li>
  <li>No collateral required</li>
  <li>No personal guarantee in most cases</li>
  <li>Remittance is a small % of daily/weekly revenue — you pay as you earn</li>
</ul>

<div class="tip-box">
  <p class="tip-label">Pro tip</p>
  <p>Use their own language back at them. If they said "I don't want to go into debt," say: "What you're describing is exactly what this isn't — it's a revenue partnership, not a fixed debt obligation."</p>
</div>
