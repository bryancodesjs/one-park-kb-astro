<h2>Why they say it</h2>
<p>This is a legitimate objection — many small businesses are family-run. The key is validating it without letting it become a dead end. Your goal is either to get the partner on the call now, or to arm your prospect with everything they need to "sell" it internally.</p>

<div class="script-box">
  <p class="script-label">Say this (try to get them on now)</p>
  <p>"Absolutely — big decisions should include everyone. Is there any chance your [husband/wife/partner] is available for just 10 minutes right now? I want to make sure they hear the same information and have their questions answered too."</p>
</div>

<h2>If partner can't join now</h2>
<div class="script-box">
  <p class="script-label">Say this</p>
  <p>"Totally understand. Let me ask — what would your partner's main questions be? Let me answer those now so you walk into that conversation fully prepared."</p>
</div>

<h2>Common partner concerns to pre-address</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>What does it cost?</strong> → Explain the factor rate and total payback amount clearly.</li>
  <li><strong>What if business slows down?</strong> → Remittances flex with revenue — slower month = smaller payment.</li>
  <li><strong>Is this a loan?</strong> → RBF is structured differently. No fixed monthly payment, no collateral required.</li>
  <li><strong>Is OPF legitimate?</strong> → Trustpilot 4.8, BBB A+, Google 4.5, funded 55,000+ businesses since 2010.</li>
</ul>

<div class="tip-box">
  <p class="tip-label">Pro tip</p>
  <p>Before ending the call, send them the OPF overview email so they have something tangible to share with their partner. Then lock in a specific callback time that includes the partner.</p>
</div>
