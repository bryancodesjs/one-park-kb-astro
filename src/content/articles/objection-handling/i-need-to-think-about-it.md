<h2>Why they say it</h2>
<p>"I need to think about it" almost never means the client is actually going away to research your offer. It usually signals one of three things: they don't have enough information, they feel mild pressure and need breathing room, or they haven't yet connected the funding to a real, urgent business need.</p>

<h2>The goal</h2>
<p>Don't fight the objection — agree with it, then ask a clarifying question that brings the conversation back to their need. You want to surface the <em>real</em> hesitation.</p>

<div class="script-box">
  <p class="script-label">Say this</p>
  <p>"Of course — this is a big decision and I want you to feel completely comfortable. While I have you, can I ask: what specific questions would you want answered in that thinking time? I'd love to make sure you have everything you need right now."</p>
</div>

<h2>If they give you a reason</h2>
<p>Now you have something to work with. Address the specific concern directly using the relevant objection guide (rates, spouse, past declines, etc.).</p>

<h2>If they stay vague</h2>
<div class="script-box">
  <p class="script-label">Say this</p>
  <p>"I totally understand. A lot of our clients felt the same way at first. What I can tell you is that the business owners who moved forward typically say the biggest regret was waiting. Can I ask — is there anything holding you back beyond just timing?"</p>
</div>

<div class="tip-box">
  <p class="tip-label">Pro tip</p>
  <p>Silence is your friend after asking a clarifying question. Give the client 3–5 seconds to respond before you speak again. Filling that silence yourself is the #1 mistake on stall objections.</p>
</div>

<h2>If they insist on calling back</h2>
<p>Secure the follow-up with specifics — not "I'll call you back," but a confirmed time.</p>
<div class="script-box">
  <p class="script-label">Say this</p>
  <p>"No problem at all. Let's set a time so I can hold your spot and make sure nothing changes in the meantime. Does [tomorrow at 10am] work, or is [Thursday afternoon] better?"</p>
</div>
