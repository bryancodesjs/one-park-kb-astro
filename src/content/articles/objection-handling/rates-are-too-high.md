<h2>Why this objection comes up</h2>
<p>Most clients are comparing our factor rate to a bank's APR — two completely different structures. A bank APR of 8% sounds better than a 1.3 factor rate, but they're not comparable. Your job is to educate, not defend.</p>

<div class="warning-box">
  <p class="warning-label">Important</p>
  <p>Never quote an APR equivalent for our products. Factor rates and APR are different instruments. Quoting a converted APR is both misleading and a compliance violation.</p>
</div>

<h2>Reframe: factor rate vs. APR</h2>
<div class="script-box">
  <p class="script-label">Say this</p>
  <p>"I completely understand the concern — and I want to make sure we're comparing the right things. Our financing uses a factor rate, which works differently from a bank's APR. With a factor rate, you know your exact total payback from day one. There are no compounding surprises, no prepayment penalties. If you receive $50,000 at a 1.3 factor rate, your total payback is $65,000 — period."</p>
</div>

<h2>Shift to the cost of not having capital</h2>
<div class="script-box">
  <p class="script-label">Say this</p>
  <p>"Let me ask you something — what's the cost of not having this capital right now? If you're turning down orders, understaffed during your busy season, or watching a competitor take your customers — what does that cost your business per month?"</p>
</div>

<h2>Speed premium</h2>
<p>Banks can take 60–90 days and still say no. We can get funds in the client's account in 72 hours. That speed has value — especially when opportunity has a shelf life.</p>

<div class="tip-box">
  <p class="tip-label">Pro tip</p>
  <p>If the client has a specific number in mind ("I won't pay more than X%"), ask where that number came from. Often it's a bank quote they got months ago, or something they read online. That context helps you respond specifically.</p>
</div>
