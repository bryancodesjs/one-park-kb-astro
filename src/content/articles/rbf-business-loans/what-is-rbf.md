<h2>The simple version</h2>
<p>Revenue-Based Financing (RBF) is a way for a business to receive a lump sum of capital upfront in exchange for agreeing to remit a small, fixed percentage of their daily or weekly revenue until the total agreed amount is paid back.</p>

<h2>How it's different from a loan</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>No fixed monthly payment</strong> — remittance adjusts with your revenue</li>
  <li><strong>No compounding interest</strong> — uses a factor rate (a flat multiplier), not APR</li>
  <li><strong>No collateral required</strong> — based on business revenue, not assets</li>
  <li><strong>Faster to fund</strong> — as little as 72 hours vs. weeks or months at a bank</li>
</ul>

<h2>The factor rate explained</h2>
<p>Instead of interest, RBF uses a factor rate — a simple multiplier applied once to the funded amount.</p>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Example:</strong> $50,000 funded × 1.3 factor rate = <strong>$65,000 total payback</strong></li>
  <li>The client knows exactly what they owe from day one</li>
  <li>No surprises, no compounding</li>
</ul>
