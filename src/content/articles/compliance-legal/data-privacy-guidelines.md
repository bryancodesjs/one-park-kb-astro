<h2>What counts as sensitive data</h2>
<ul>
  <li>Social Security Numbers (SSN)</li>
  <li>Employer Identification Numbers (EIN)</li>
  <li>Bank account numbers and routing numbers</li>
  <li>Personal financial information (revenue, debts, credit score)</li>
  <li>Date of birth</li>
</ul>

<h2>Rules for handling sensitive data</h2>
<ul>
  <li>Collect sensitive data only through authorized channels (Salesforce, secure forms)</li>
  <li>Never write down SSNs or account numbers on paper or in unsecured notes</li>
  <li>Never repeat sensitive numbers out loud unnecessarily — if a client gives you an SSN verbally, confirm by reading back only the last 4 digits</li>
  <li>Do not share client information with anyone not involved in processing their file</li>
  <li>Log all client information into Salesforce promptly and accurately</li>
</ul>

<div class="warning-box">
  <p class="warning-label">GLBA Compliance</p>
  <p>OPF is subject to the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA), which governs how financial companies protect client information. Mishandling client data is a federal compliance issue — not just an internal policy violation.</p>
</div>
