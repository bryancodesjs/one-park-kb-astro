<h2>What gets evaluated</h2>
<p>All calls are subject to quality review. Evaluators listen for:</p>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Opening:</strong> Proper greeting, name, company, recording disclosure</li>
  <li><strong>Discovery:</strong> Qualifying questions asked, active listening demonstrated</li>
  <li><strong>Product knowledge:</strong> Accurate description of RBF, factor rates, and requirements</li>
  <li><strong>Objection handling:</strong> Professional, empathetic responses — no pressure tactics</li>
  <li><strong>Compliance:</strong> No prohibited statements, proper disclosures made</li>
  <li><strong>Closing:</strong> Clear next steps communicated, follow-up set if needed</li>
  <li><strong>Salesforce logging:</strong> Call logged within 5 minutes with accurate disposition and notes</li>
</ul>

<h2>Metrics you'll be tracked on</h2>
<ul>
  <li>Contact rate (% of leads successfully reached)</li>
  <li>Conversion rate (% of contacted leads moved to funding manager)</li>
  <li>Average handle time (target varies by team — ask your lead)</li>
  <li>QA score (monthly average from call reviews)</li>
</ul>

<div class="tip-box">
  <p class="tip-label">Fastest way to improve QA scores</p>
  <p>Record yourself on a practice call and listen back. Most agents hear things they didn't realize they were saying — filler words, rushing, or skipping the disclosure. Self-review once a week is the single highest-leverage habit for score improvement.</p>
</div>
