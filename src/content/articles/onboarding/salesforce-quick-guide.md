<h2>Before the call</h2>
<ul>
  <li>Open the lead record in Salesforce and review any prior notes or call history</li>
  <li>Check the lead source and any pre-filled data from the web form</li>
  <li>Note any prior contact attempts and outcomes</li>
</ul>

<h2>During the call</h2>
<ul>
  <li>Take notes in real-time in the Salesforce activity log (or your note pad if typing is distracting)</li>
  <li>Do not leave Salesforce fields blank — partial data is better than no data</li>
</ul>

<h2>After every call — required fields to update</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Call Disposition:</strong> Connected / No Answer / Left Voicemail / Wrong Number / etc.</li>
  <li><strong>Lead Status:</strong> Update to reflect where the client is in the funnel</li>
  <li><strong>Next Activity Date:</strong> Set a follow-up task if the client needs a callback</li>
  <li><strong>Notes:</strong> Summary of the conversation, objections raised, client's stated need, and any commitments made</li>
  <li><strong>Monthly Revenue:</strong> If collected during discovery</li>
  <li><strong>Industry:</strong> If not already populated</li>
</ul>

<div class="tip-box">
  <p class="tip-label">Log within 5 minutes</p>
  <p>Memory degrades fast. Log call notes while the conversation is fresh — before your next call comes in. Your funding manager reads these notes to continue the relationship; your accuracy directly impacts their success.</p>
</div>
